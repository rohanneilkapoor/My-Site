Overview

Hosted in UCLA’s iconic arena, Pauley Pavilion, LA Hacks is the largest hackathon in the nation. In the past its largest event hosted 1,500 hackers and guest speakers including Founder and CEO of Snapchat, Evan Spiegel, Co-Founder of Reddit, Alexis Ohanian, eight-time Olympic medalist, Apolo Ohno, and President of Y Combinator, Sam Altman.

Research

Hacker Experience

Hacker experience is the entirety of the participant’s experience from the moment he/she walks in the door to the moment he/she leaves. This is the foundation for how we measure our success.

Laying a Foundation

Before doing any branding, I wanted to lay a foundation structured around the purpose of LA Hacks and what we hope to achieve this year. Because hacker experience is the direct measurement of success, I started with studying all of the "end-of-hackathon" surveys we have hackers fill out.

I also spoke with numerous hackers, both during and after last year’s event, about how they view LA Hacks, what they liked about our event, what they don’t like, etc. I made an effort to receive opinions from both young/new hackers as well as experienced hackers. I also sifted through previous sponsor reviews. Although the hackathon is about the hackers, there is no hackathon without our sponsors, thus their happiness is just as important. The two most common forms of critical feedback were:

  • There were a lot of people at the event who weren’t hacking
  • The hacks from last year weren’t that impressive
Source: LA Hacks, Hackathon Event 2016 Google Sheet

From there I conducted interviews with previous organizers and current organizers. The goal of each interview was to better understand what each organizer wants LA Hacks to be. The most common answer was something along the lines of the following:

Goal: To provide an environment where hackers from all backgrounds and experience levels can come together to learn from one another and inevitably build something both useful and meaningful to the best of their abilities.

Clearly there was a major disconnect between what the organizers wanted and what the hackers saw.

Logistical Solutions

Because I was a Director of Operations last year, I thought a lot about how we could solve these problems from an operations perspective with the entire team. We came up with the following solutions:

Setting the Date

We noticed that most of the people who came to LA Hacks for non-hacking reasons were students from the UC system. Thus, we decided to host the hackathon, the weekend at the end of spring break right before spring quarter starts. This way, we hoped that only the people who genuinely wanted to hack would come.

Data Hacks

We introduced a track for data hackers that would be judged separately from all other hacks. This is a rapidly growing field and we felt that if we provided a specific platform for those interested in a specific field, it would cause excitement among those in that community.

Installations

At the start of the hackathon, there is a lot of down time before the hacking begins. Because hacker experience is defined as the entirety of the participant’s experience as soon as he/she walks in the doors, we needed to make better use of this time. We decided to have design/tech-related installations for the participants to interact with both for entertainment and inspiration.

Branding

From there, I thought about how to address the problems introduced above with our brand. How could we represent ourselves to show that we are welcoming, yet serious about putting on a hackathon where all participants are hacking and building something useful and meaningful.

Branding Research

Like I said earlier, I met all different kinds of people and talked to them about how they currently viewed LA Hacks and where they want it to go. For brand purposes, I spoke with the organizers about how they wanted the LA Hacks brand to be perceived. A lot was said and I narrowed it down to the following 4 themes that would encompass the entirety of the brand:

  • community-based
  • approachable
  • professional
  • empowering

Notice how there’s no mention of technology. That’s because LA Hacks isn’t about the latest advancements in VR or data science. It’s about coming together as a community to learn from others and solve problems by building something meaningful and useful. Technology is simply the means in which it's accomplished.
