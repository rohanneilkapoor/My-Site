Project

I worked on the team responsible for lending and handling small business loans. Because this is a relatively new service for American Express, my team was rapidly testing and monitoring borrower and monetary trends. To do this, we worked with managers to decide on important trends and design and build interactive data visualization applications.

I worked with 2 other engineers to create 5 of these applications. I worked in the entire life cycle from discussing important trends, to querying relevant data, and then designing and building the visualizations and applications.

Tools: JavaScript, HTML/CSS for UI, and Splunk and it's native language for the data querying and basic visualization development (Although Spunk has many pre-developed visualization formats, they did not suite all of our needs, requiring further custom visualization development).

Hackathon

Prompt: Solve a problem that either American Express customers or employees are facing.

The problem my team chose to solve was the confusion that credit card customers face when attempting to navigate and make sense of their previous transaction history trends.

Our solution was a complete re-design of the American Express credit card transaction section of the application with an emphasis on data visualizations. Because this was only a 6 hour hackathon, we weren’t able to perform much research other than confirming the need based on each other’s responses and a discussion of the major pain points. As a result, we built a number of features/visualizations. The three most well received were the following:

Expenses Timeline

Problem: American Express cardholders initially have a difficult time understanding their spending trends. It's difficult to see how one is currently spending compared to a previous time period. This makes budgeting much more challenging.

Solution: We built an interactive line graph visualization showing a timeline of a card user’s expenses in weekly, monthly, and yearly terms.

Transaction Map

Problem: Credit card users sometimes have a hard time remembering where and when a specific transaction took place. Also, fraud detection isn’t perfect and is not always reported correctly by banks or credit card companies.

Solution: We utilized the Google Maps API to display a map of a card user’s recent expenses. We emphasized the pins on the map that correlated with more recent transactions. The transactions that took place a while ago were less emphasized. This had two purposes:

  • This simplifies the data and presents it in a way that’s easier to grasp.
  • If a user is able to see that a transaction took place in an area he/she was not at during a specific time, he/she will more easily be able to recognize fraudulent use of his/her card.

A Cleaner UI

We implemented a much simpler UI by placing all transactions on a physical timeline, making it easier for card holders to mentally keep track of previous transactions. We also emphasized transaction amounts.

As a result we were named winner of the hackathon. Also, a visualization very similar to our expenses timeline visualization was actually launched on the American Express credit card application around the same time of our hackathon. We obviously weren't the source of this, but we like to joke that we were :)
