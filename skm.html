SKM Industries Inc.

WEB DESIGN STRATEGY & DEVELOPMENT

Overview

Background

SKM Industries Inc. is a CNC machining company based in Valencia, CA. The company does contractual manufacturing for organizations in a wide variety of industries including as aerospace, medical, and commercial. Last summer SKM Industries approached me to design and build a responsive and mobile friendly company website that would differentiate itself from its competition.

Achievements

  • Designed and built a fully responsive and lead-converting website seen here
  • After the site launch, SKM Industries was also able to secure work with a number of new clients including Mitsubishi, Hyperloop One, and University of California, Irvine
  • The site saw a 600% increase in the number of new order requests within the first week of the launch.
  • SKM Industries saw an influx in job applicants

Problem

The CNC machining industry is incredibly competitive, especially in the Los Angeles area. Most company websites in the industry show very few differentiating services and features, and each site itself feels incredibly outdated. SKM Industries’ website was no exception. Also, during this time, SKM Industries was struggling to find work because:

  • Its main client, a company that provided SKM Industries with over half of its work, was struggling financially
  • The machining industry was affected because of an economic downturn

Solution

Differentiate SKM Industries from its competitors so that it can secure a diverse set of clients.

Research

Confirming the Need

Unfortunately, because it’s current website was so outdated, there were no analytics or other tools set up to tell me about their traffic. Thus, I conducted interviews with the client and other similar machine shops. I reached out to both machine shops with outdated and non-outdated websites. We discussed what companies look for in a machine shop when looking to get contracting work done. Because these companies also hire other machine shops to do some contracting work, they provided answers as both the buyer and the seller. Furthermore, we discussed what one looks for on a machine shop’s website.

I found that buyers almost always visit the machine shop’s websites looking for a Portfolio and Capabilities section. Also, as with many other industries, buyers look for reputation. If a company has done impressive work with well-known companies, they’re more likely to secure more clients.

Client Research

In order to differentiate SKM from it’s competition, I interviewed the client numerous times looking for differentiating features. Since the Portfolio and Capabilities sections were the most important, I searched for more information about the company that would help me make these sections more unique when compared to competitors. The following were mutually agreed to be the most important differentiating features to display on the website:

  • Most shops use paper to send information within the shop. The client built its own custom software that uses machine mounted iPads to send information.
  • The client does production, short run, and assembly work. Very few of its competitors do all three categories of work.
  • The client's facility runs off of 70% solar energy due to rooftop-mounted solar panels.
  • The client has done work for big companies/organizations such as Lockheed Martin, Caltech, and Haas.
  • Throughout its existence, the client has done work for companies in 7 different industries.

Website Goal

Convince the visitor to request a quote/get in contact with the client by either filling out a form or directly contacting the shop themselves.

UX

I wanted the site to be incredibly simple in terms on navigation. The idea was that the user lands on the page and only needs to scroll down and click to see everything. Because the capabilities and portfolio section required a bit more information, I put them into modals instead of separate pages. That way a user can scroll down the main page, click a button to get to the capabilities modal, and then exit the modal to find himself/herself back at the same place on the main page. No page refresh or extra scrolling. The experience should be one smooth flow.

UI

Because of branding and logo constraints, I couldn't alter the main red. So I grouped it with other calmer colors to give the palette a more modern feel.
